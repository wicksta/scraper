## Proposed Development

Planning permission is sought for:

**“External alterations to the ground floor office reception façade and entrance, including the installation of an additional fire escape door to the Whitcomb Street elevation and replacement of the existing automated revolving door with a reduced-height manually operable revolving door, with associated glazing and façade adjustments.”**

The proposals relate specifically to the **ground floor reception** serving the office accommodation on the upper floors. No other parts of the building (including retail accommodation) form part of this application and there is **no change of use**.

## The Site and Surroundings

The Site comprises a mixed-use commercial building with **retail at ground floor** and **office uses above**, located immediately to the west of Leicester Square within the West End. The building is **not statutorily or locally listed**, but it lies within the **Leicester Square Conservation Area**. The Site also falls within the **Central Activities Zone (CAZ)**, the **West End Town Centre**, the **West End Retail and Leisure Special Policy Area**, an **Archaeological Priority Area (Tier I)**, and **Flood Zone 1**.

The surrounding area is characterised by a vibrant mix of commercial uses including offices, restaurants, bars, hotels and leisure/entertainment uses, reflecting the established role and function of this part of Westminster’s CAZ.

## Planning History (Summary)

The planning history is extensive. The Design and Access Statement summarises the most relevant permissions, including the comprehensive redevelopment/refurbishment of the building (ref. **12/07691/FULL**) and subsequent approvals relating to detailed design and amendments to the ground floor façades and office entrance (including refs. **14/00337/NMA**, **13/11967/FULL**, and **15/04834/NMA**). The current proposals represent a further, targeted refinement to the reception entrance and Whitcomb Street façade to address operational and life-safety constraints identified through use of the building.

## Planning Assessment and Justification

The statutory development plan includes the **Westminster City Plan 2019–2040 (January 2026)** and the **London Plan (2021)**. The **NPPF (2024)** is also a material consideration. The Design and Access Statement identifies the key policy considerations; in City Plan terms, the proposals are particularly aligned with:

- **Policy 18 – Town Centres, High Streets and the CAZ**
- **Policy 42 – Design Principles** (including people-centred design, inclusive access, safety and security)
- **Policy 44 – Westminster’s Heritage**
- **Policy 45 – Townscape and Architecture**

A summary of the principal planning considerations is set out below.

### 1. Design, townscape and people-centred outcomes (Policies 42 and 45)

The proposals are modest, localised alterations to the ground floor reception frontage and entrance, responding to identified operational issues with the existing entrance arrangement and to the building’s fire-escape strategy.

- **Revolving door replacement:** The existing automated revolving door has experienced persistent mechanical failures and, due to its height, is difficult to operate manually during malfunction. The proposed replacement is **reduced in height** and **manually operable**, while maintaining a consistent design approach and materiality (bronze anodised aluminium and clear glazing) to ensure continuity with the existing façade composition. This is a practical improvement that supports a safer, more reliable and inclusive entrance arrangement, consistent with the people-centred objectives of **Policy 42**.

- **Additional fire escape door:** The introduction of an additional door to the Whitcomb Street elevation is driven by the need to provide a more robust and independent means of escape for upper floor office occupiers, enabling the reception to function as a welcoming arrival space rather than an overly constrained “sterile” area. The door is designed to sit discreetly within the existing curtain wall system, with associated localised adjustments to glazing, transoms and mullions to ensure alignment and a coherent façade rhythm. The Design and Access Statement confirms the intention to match and complement the existing façade elements, minimising visual impact and ensuring the elevation remains well-composed, in accordance with **Policy 45**.

Overall, the proposals represent a proportionate and well-resolved set of façade and entrance alterations which improve functionality and user experience, while maintaining a high-quality architectural response appropriate to this prominent West End location.

### 2. Heritage and conservation area considerations (Policy 44)

Although the building is unlisted, the Site lies within the **Leicester Square Conservation Area**. The proposals have been developed to ensure that the character and appearance of the conservation area is conserved.

In particular:

- the replacement revolving door is designed to **closely replicate the appearance** of the existing installation in terms of materiality and overall proportions; and
- the additional fire escape door is integrated into the existing façade system and is intended to match adjacent elements, limiting the extent of change to the Whitcomb Street elevation.

Given the limited scope of works, their careful integration, and the continuity of materials and façade language, the proposals will **preserve the character and appearance** of the Leicester Square Conservation Area and are consistent with **Policy 44**.

### 3. CAZ / Town Centre function (Policy 18)

The proposals support the continued successful operation and attractiveness of office accommodation within the CAZ and West End Town Centre. By improving the entrance experience and enabling a higher quality reception environment (as explained in the Design and Access Statement), the scheme will assist in maintaining the competitiveness and appeal of commercial floorspace in this location, consistent with the objectives of **Policy 18**.

### 4. Amenity and environmental considerations

The proposals are confined to the ground floor reception façade and entrance and do not introduce new uses or intensify activity beyond the established office reception function. The works are therefore not expected to result in adverse impacts on neighbouring amenity. The proposals also support safer circulation and egress arrangements, aligning with the safety and wellbeing aims embedded within **Policy 42**.
